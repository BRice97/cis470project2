CloudComputing

Atlanta, GA - Thursday March 9, 2017
Key Facts & Importance

Summary

What Is It Cloud Computing is the storing and accessing of data and programs over the Internet instead of a computer's hard drive. The word 'cloud' is a metaphor to the Internet

Major Examples Google Drive, Apple iCloud, Amazon Cloud, DropBox.

All of these major examples of cloud computing share that they offer the option to sync your files with a local storage over a the Internet. Synchronization is a cornerstone of the cloud computing experience, even if you do access the file locally.

Why Cloud Computing is the Best Way to Access & Store Data?

Response

The difference between accessing and storing data locally on a hard drive and through the cloud, is that a person can only access what they need locally through the hard drive or local network. Through the cloud, a person can access their data from any computer regardless of what network the computer runs on. Cloud computing allows college students, and other people in the corporate world, to work on assignments remotely from home and access those same files from a different device. Therefore, cloud computing is a very beneficial to the average person that is constantly accessing and storing files.
